Our Company

An introduction from chairman Bill Siegel

I am a collector of modern firsts and photobooks after catching the bug back in the 70s. I’ve spent a lot of time (and money) building and refining my collection over the past 40 or so years. It’s been one of the most rewarding pursuits of my life.

A few years ago, I was approached by one of the founders of Manhattan Rare Books. MRB was working on developing a networking site for collectors and asked me if I would help with ideas and financing. It sounded interesting so I got in.

I’ve bought books from some of the best dealers in the world, on both sides of the Atlantic. In the old days I bought books the old fashioned way — from catalogues, from direct quote, and in person at book fairs and in dealer’s shops.

The internet brought a lot of new dealers into the market, some good, some not so good. Now it’s easy for a dealer to open an online shop. That’s good and bad. I’ve always been willing to pay a little (or even a lot more) for top quality from reputable dealers. On CF you will find a smaller number of dealers than you will find on other sites, and fewer books. The idea is to help fellow collectors find top quality dealers and top quality books. Sometimes less is more.

How I like to use CollectorsFolio

  1. I use my profile as a kind of business card. It tells dealers what my interests are and lets them know that I’m a pretty serious collector. This allows me to connect with dealers online without having to introduce myself over and over. Furthermore, my profile does not display my personal information, which means I can make inquiries anonymously when I want. This is not a bad thing.

  2. I have sold some of my books directly to fellow collectors. In time this could get more interesting. I don’t want to be responsible for shipping or taking payments but in theory we collectors could set up our own deals and pay a brokerage fee to make transactions go smoothly.

  3. We all have an interest in seeing the rare book market grow. This means reaching new, younger collectors who can carry the torch (and buy our books when we’re ready to sell). CF is interactive and social — just like a book fair but accessible from your laptop or mobile device.

  4. Anyone who has a list of books or want lists can post it to the site (free). It’s easy and, in the aggregate, could be interesting to see what’s out there.

  5. Connecting: I’ve been collecting for 35 years and I still enjoy going to a book fair and meeting dealers I don’t yet know. I love to browse for books and I love to build relationships with serious dealers (and a few collectors) who share my interests… CF makes this easier

  6. Book collecting is cool in person — A list celebs show up to book fairs — but not so much online. We’re trying to change that.
