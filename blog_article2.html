Overview

Walt Whitman (1819-1892). Known for his barbaric yawp; the original Brooklyn hipster. Good material available from $750-$200,000. Signed editions of Leaves of Grass available at $6000-9000. Dramatic price gains for Leaves of Grass since 1965.

Gifts & entry level

The first printing of the1892 Deathbed edition is a book worth owning. It sells for about $1000, it’s the last edition that Whitman himself worked on and thus represents the final version of Leaves of Grass. A handsome octavo volume bound in green cloth; usually obtainable in very nice condition and presents well. Does not need a custom box but if you put it in one (approx $300-500 extra) it will seem extra special sitting on a bookshelf or desk. The first printing is quite rare.

Other options:

  • The third edition of Leaves of Grass, published five years after the first edition. Contains 122 new obtainable in fairly good shape — but not a particularly elegant binding.
  • A facsimile edition of Leaves of Grass, published by Thomas Bird Mosher in 1919. A very nice book. Copies available from Andrew Cahan and others at about $1250.

Best of the best

A first issue of the first edition of Leaves of Grass in the original binding. Truly exceptional copies will have the original green cloth with the original gilt preserved. New York dealer James Cummins offers a beautiful copy at ,000. If you can live without the original binding you can have a first edition on your bookshelf for ,000 (Rootenberg). If you are willing to pay for a premium copy, look for copies in unsoiled original cloth with bright gilt and inner hinges that are reasonably sound (lesser copies will have hinge repairs or will have been resewn). Expect the portrait of Whitman to be somewhat foxed. Some very interesting copies have surfaced over the years, including Thoreau’s own copy (sold by Buddenbrooks a few years back) and a copy of the fourth edition with a intriguingly perfunctory inscription: “Algernon Charles Swinburne, from Walt Whitman.”

OTHER NOTABLE TITLES & EDITIONS

  • 1842. Franklin Evans; Or the Inebriate. Whitman’s first separate publication (appeared in “The New World,” Vol. II, No. 10 Extra Series, Number 34). A copy sold in 1972 for $2800 (Sotheby Park-Bernet).
  • 1856. Leaves of Grass (2nd edition). With 20 poems not included in the 1855 edition. A copy in original cloth currently offered by Raptis Rare Books for $15,000. Sotheby’s sold a copy that once belonged to Whitman’s sister for $23,750 in 2014.
  • 1865. Drum Taps. The most important work of poetry on the American Civil War.
  • 1876. Leaves of Grass (‘Author’s Edition’). 1/600 copies signed by Whitman. Not uncommon to see these with presentation inscriptions ‘from the Author’. Second Life Books offers a copy for $13,750; Peter Harrington for $9800.
  • 1882. Leaves of Grass (‘Author’s Edition’, yet again, but just under 100 copies this time). Signed by Whitman.
